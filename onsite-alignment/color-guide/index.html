Working with the walls you have

Match, or contrast — on purpose

The wall is the frame around the frame. Two quick ways to decide.

Read the wall

Start from the colour that's already there.

White / light grayAnything — let the art lead.
Warm neutralsEarthy, soft-contrast tones read curated.
Navy / charcoalHigh contrast — whites and warm brights.
PastelMuted or monochrome keeps it calm.
Brick / woodNature-led or industrial monochromes.

Choose the feeling

Decide the piece's job before its palette.

A statementBold colour, strong contrast, one focal point.
Something calmMonochrome, soft edges, natural texture.
EnergyBright accents, dynamic line, warm tones.
GroundingDark neutrals, centered, organic shape.
An accentA tone pulled from a cushion or rug.

A simple rule for balance

60% · Dominant — walls, floors
30% · Secondary — furniture
10% · Accent

FAQ

Common questions

What color art works best in small rooms?
Light or monochrome tones keep a small room feeling open. A single quiet piece usually does more than several busy ones.
Can I mix bold wall colors and bold artwork?
Yes, if you balance it. Share a tone between the wall and the piece, or let a neutral mat give the eye somewhere to rest.
What is a safe choice if I'm unsure?
Black-and-white or earth-toned artworks are flexible and hold up over time.
