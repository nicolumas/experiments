Two Yeses · flow overview

Every screen, annotated

The complete anonymous, no-login flow at a glance. Each frame is the live prototype opened directly to that screen, with the mechanics noted beside it. Nobody signs in and nothing is saved to a wishlist; the viewing itself is the identity.

Phase 01 · Find

It starts on the product page

The veto-in-the-kitchen happens on a PDP. The entry lives right there, beside the wishlist heart.

Initiator

Product page

"Decide together" sits under the buy actions, quieter than the wishlist heart. It shows on every product page, signed in or not. No login prompt, ever.

Phase 02 · Compose

Three works, without saved works

The initiator arrives with one work already chosen, and swipes to pick two more from sources every anonymous session already has.

Initiator

Compose

Recently viewed is the default source; Similar is seeded from the entry work. Swipe the carousel, tap to add. If signed in, the wishlist appears as one more source, never a gate.

Initiator

Seal

Mark the one you'd live with and write a single line. On seal it is written to the viewing record and never returned by the partner's link until both commit. This is what makes the reveal genuine.

Phase 03 · Share

Handing it to the other person

Sealing mints two role-scoped links. This screen is only about getting the invitation across.

Initiator

Share

Send opens the native share sheet, straight into Messages or WhatsApp. Copy link for anywhere else. Hand over keeps the same-device ritual.

Hand-over

The perspective changes

A deliberate seam. From here you are the partner, not the initiator. On one device it's a physical hand-over; on two, the moment they open their link. The sealed answer stays hidden.

Initiator

The way back

If sent rather than handed over, the initiator waits. Their own link holds the partner's answer until it's committed. An optional, skippable email is the only place an address is asked. This is what login used to do.

Phase 04 · Open, view, commit

The partner opens the link

No account, ever. The token in the link is their whole identity — enough to view, vote, and write one sentence.

Partner

Invitation

Opening the link is the reply. Three works, chosen for the two of you, one answer already sealed inside.

Partner

The viewing

One work at a time on a charcoal void, shown full and uncropped. The reveal is the product — the opposite of a thumbnail grid.

Partner

Ballot

Mark one work and write a line, still blind to the sealed answer. "None of these" is an honest option, not an error. On Commit, state turns to committed and each link may read the other's answer.

Phase 05 · Reveal

The exchange resolves

A pure function of the two picks. Same work is a match; different works open the concession round, then a diptych or the meeting point.

Both of you

Match

The same work, chosen twice, apart. Both sentences appear together under it — then a quiet handoff to acquire via a guest cart.

Both of you

Concession round

A mismatch isn't a dead end. Each sees only the other's work and sentence, and may concede or hold. Blind on both sides again.

Both of you

Decided

One gave way. The work that stands is the one that was held; both sentences hang with it.

Both of you

Diptych

Both conceded. You each surrendered to the other's — so the wall gets the pair.

Both of you

Meeting point

Both held. The collection answers with one work between your choices, from the similarity backend, filtered to in-stock. A yes from each, or a quiet close. No push, ever.

Both of you

Two yeses, after all

Neither chose it alone; both chose it together. The rarest, warmest ending.
