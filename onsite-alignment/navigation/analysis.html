How to read this — and its one honest limit

GA4 can't tie a specific nav click to a specific order, so this isn't click-to-purchase attribution. It compares share of attention (nav clicks) with share of revenue (DWH web order value) on the axes where the two genuinely join.

The nav is organised by technique (Fotografie, Malerei) and theme (Abstrakt, Wasser); the DWH doesn't measure revenue on those axes at all. It measures by artist, edition tier and product type. So the clean join is artist (Top 20 + Artists in Focus) — shown in full below — plus a few edition/format items. Technique- and theme-level revenue can't be answered until order lines carry a technique tag.

The headline

The right artists — clicked in the wrong proportions.

The artists LUMAS features in the nav drive ~60% of web revenue: good curation. But attention is badly misallocated across them. Gerhard Richter alone takes 35% of nav-artist clicks yet 6% of their revenue, while Beatrice Hug — the single highest-earning artist at €214k — gets under 2% of the clicks. The image-tile grid starves the very artists who sell most; the famous-name text links pull clicks that don't convert in proportion.

What to do with this

  • Rework the Top 20 tile grid. It holds real revenue leaders but they barely get clicked — and it omits several top earners (Pedro Blas, Damien Hirst, Ai Weiwei, Katharina Grosse). Either surface the high-earning tile artists better or rebuild the selection around revenue, not just reputation.
  • Treat Gerhard Richter as a hook, not a workhorse. He's the biggest single click magnet in the whole nav yet converts modestly (5 SKUs, €108k). Fine as a marquee name — just don't read his clicks as buying intent.
  • You can't revenue-validate the technique/theme nav yet. Deciding whether to keep or cut Fotografie vs Malerei, or Abstrakt vs Wasser, needs a technique/motif tag on order lines (or a SKU→technique map). That's the single highest-value tracking fix here.
  • Merchandising tiles look justified. SALE and Neuheiten dominate clicks, and revenue concentrates in Limited Edition (83.5%) and premium finishes — consistent with high-intent shoppers using those shortcuts. No case to remove them.
  • Fix the artist-tile tagging. ~480 Top 20 tile clicks still log a whitespace label, so per-artist attention is undercounted — the misalignment above is directional, and would sharpen with clean tags.
  • Nav clicks are lumas.de web only. Revenue here is the web channel (€2.90M); a large share of LUMAS revenue is offline/gallery and outside this surface entirely.
