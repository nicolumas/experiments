PDP UX Audit, condensed

Three fixable problems, three live concepts. Built on the real product page (Marta Contreras Simó, Liu), in Archivo.

The verdict

The image-led layout works. The friction is the interaction layer on top of the artwork: three control clusters, three styles, three corners, with no shared system. Worst case is the binoculars, which is unlabeled, hover-only and wrong-metaphor, so “discover more” is undiscoverable (and dead on touch). And “Limited Edition 150” states a number instead of selling the scarcity. All three map to known heuristics. Ship the labels and parity fix now; A/B the wording and the unified rail.

The three problems

SEV 1

Controls scattered across 3 corners, 3 styles

Why it hurtsNo grouping cue, so users can’t inventory what’s possible; bare icons read as décor.
Consistency #4Gestalt proximityFitts
Fix → one system, one style, all labeled (Concepts A→B→C).
SEV 2

Binoculars / “More like this”

Why it hurtsUnlabeled + hover-only = invisible on touch; binoculars reads as “zoom,” not “similar.”
Recognition #6Real-world match #2Visibility #1
Fix → persistent label and a clearer metaphor. Best is a text chip, “Discover more like this.”
SEV 2

“Limited Edition 150” undersells the USP

Why it hurtsBuried mid-line at metadata weight; states a fact, triggers no scarcity/prestige.
Visual hierarchyScarcityArt vernacular #2
Fix → own line + badge, art-standard wording “Edition of 100.”
