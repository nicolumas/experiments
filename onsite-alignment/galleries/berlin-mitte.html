What your visit will feel like

A gallery you can wander into.

A LUMAS visit is meant to be unhurried. You can come, look around, and leave again. If you want help, ask. If you don't, browse in peace.

Browse in peace.

Take your time. No one will approach you unless you signal you'd like company.

Hear the stories.

Our advisors love to talk about the artists, the editions, and the work behind each piece.

See it on your wall.

Bring a photo of the room. We'll mock up any work at the right size before you commit.

Take it home today.

Most works on the wall are in stock, framed, and ready to leave with you.

Walk-through on request.

Ask for a curator tour of the current hang. It takes about twenty minutes.

60 days to live with it.

Hang it. Sit with it. If it doesn't fit, return it to any LUMAS gallery, no questions asked.

Honest answers.

The questions we hear most often, written plainly. If you're wondering it, we've probably been asked it.

Do I need an appointment?

No. Walk in any time during opening hours. Spontaneous visits are just as welcome as planned ones.

Will someone approach me the moment I walk in?

No. We give you space to look. If you have a question or want advice, just ask — we'll be nearby.

Are the prices visible?

Yes. Every work has its price labelled directly. No hidden lists, no quiet questions to the desk.

What does a consultation actually look like?

Unhurried, in your pace. We listen to what you respond to, share the stories behind the works, and help with size, framing, and placement. If you want to see something on your wall before committing, bring a photo of the room.

Can I take a work home the same day?

Often, yes. Many works are in stock, already framed, and ready to leave with you. Ask what's available in the size you want.

What if it doesn't fit at home?

You have 60 days to return it to any LUMAS gallery worldwide. No reason required.

How often does the exhibition change?

The hang rotates every four to six weeks. Different artists, different works. It's worth coming back.

Is the gallery accessible?

The Hackeschen Höfe courtyard is step-free from Rosenthaler Straße. The gallery itself is on the ground floor with a single low threshold. Please write if you'd like us to meet you at the entrance.
