Speak with our team

A few things visitors ask.

Walk-in answers to the questions we hear most often across the network. For anything gallery-specific, write or call the local team.

Do I need to book a visit?

No. Every gallery except London Mayfair is open to walk-ins during the listed hours. If you'd like guaranteed time with a director, write to the gallery and we'll hold a slot.

Are children welcome?

Yes, and we'll usually find them a small chair near the window. Strollers are easy in most spaces; ask the team if you'd like to check the layout before you come.

What languages does the team speak?

German, English, and French at most galleries. Italian, Spanish, and Mandarin at several. The team at the door will tell you who's free; we'll wait until someone you can speak with naturally is.

How do I find the closest LUMAS to me?

The directory above lists every gallery with its neighbourhood and hours. The map shows pins clustered by city. If you'd rather we suggest one based on the work you like, write a quick line and we'll point you to the right room.

Can I see a specific edition in person?

Often, yes. Most editions hang in at least one gallery, and we can usually arrange to bring a print to the gallery nearest you. Tell us the artist and title and we'll find the closest viewing.

Do I need to know about art to walk in?

Not at all. Half our visitors are buying their first artwork. The galleries are designed for unhurried looking, and the team is briefed to talk plainly about the work, not at you.

How do I get the work home?

Small framed editions go home the same day; larger pieces are hand-delivered by our white-glove team across Germany and most of Europe, with the option of installation. Ask the gallery for a quote.

What if it doesn't fit at home?

You have sixty days to return any work, framed or unframed, for a full refund. The gallery will arrange collection. Limited editions held over six months are exchanged at our discretion.
